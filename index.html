Overview

Each player chooses a character and starts with a deck of 10 Starter cards. Each turn players must attack foes, and then defend against those that weren't defeated. Defeated foes reward Luck that can be used to purchase new cards. Each player has 5 health, and can no longer play when it reaches 0. If all players have 0 health, they lose. Players win when they defeat all foes.

Player Setup

Each player selects a character, and gathers their cards. The 10 cards labeled "Starter" are used to create the starting deck. The remaining cards are shuffled, and become the Loot Deck. 3 Loot Piles are made by laying out 3 cards from the Loot Deck face up on the table.

Foe Setup

Separate foe cards by their Lv (Level) into 4 piles, and shuffle them. Build the foe deck by placing cards in the following order.
• Place 2 Lv3 cards face down on the table.
• Place 1 Lv4 card on top.
• Place 7 Lv3 cards on top.
• Place 6 Lv2 cards on top.
• Place 5 Lv1 cards on top.
• Put the remaining foes away.

Play

Players play each turn together at the same time. Each turn has four phases, and occur in this order: 1) Starting Phase 2) Attack Phase 3) Defend Phase 4) Loot Phase

1) Starting Phase

Each player draws 5 cards from their deck. If there are no cards left to draw, shuffle the discard pile to become the new deck, and continue drawing. If the board has less than 3 foes, draw them face up until it does.

2) Attack Phase

Players place their cards with (Power) face up near foes to indicate they want to damage it. Players should discuss how to split their cards between foes for the best results. Once all players agree to plan, damage is dealt. If a foe takes equal to or greater than their (Health), they are defeated and set aside.

3) Defend Phase

The foes remaining from the attack phase now damage the players. Each foe attacks each player, dealing damage equal to its . Players place their cards with (Block) face up near foes to indicate they wish to defend against it. If a player does not have enough to defend from a foe, they lose only 1 health regardless of how much Power is left unblocked.

4) Loot Phase

Each defeated foe rewards (Luck) to each player. That Luck can be spent to purchase cards from the player's Loot Piles. Players may also spend 1 Luck to reveal a Loot card and place it in a Loot Pile. When this phase is over, players may either reveal a Loot card, or fill empty Loot Piles. After the Loot Phase players return their cards to their discard pile, and defeated foes are placed in their discard pile.

Card Rules

Cards can have up to two actions that can be taken in any order, and whenever the player would like. Players may take both actions unless the cards say "or". Effects from cards only last while in play. Players may take only one of the two actions unless the card says "and". Cards always refer to the player that owns it, unless otherwise noted.

Foe Rules

If a foe requires a player to do something, each player must do it unless otherwise noted. Some foes trigger events at certain moments (When drawn, when defeated). If more than one thing would happen at the same time, players decide the order.

Stat Groups

Stat groups (, ) cannot be split between targets. Power groups can only damage 1 foe, while Block groups can only defend from 1 foe.

3-4 Player Games

3 and 4 player games have slightly different rules than a 2 player game. Instead of a 10 card starting deck, 1 card and 1 card are removed making an 8 card deck. Instead of having a hand size of 5 cards, players have 4. The Foe deck is built differently as well, with foes being placed down in the order of the following chart.

Lv.3 Lv.4 Lv.3 Lv.2 Lv.1
3 Players 2 1 8 6 4
4 Players 2 1 9 6 3

Trash

Each player has a trash pile that contains cards that are no longer in the game. When a card is trashed it is set in this pile.

Stash

When a card is stashed, it's set aside by the player, not to be returned to its discard pile. It can then be used on a later turn.

Energy

Inventors have a running score called energy. Cards give the player energy that remains for the entire game. Some cards have actions that require the player to have scored a minimum amount of energy.

Music

Musicians cards may have an action that gives (Music). Other cards may spend it to perform certain actions.

Paint

Artists have several cards with "Paint" in the name. When these cards are played with other paint cards, they are mixed and perform an action.
